Project Brief

Architectural Tectonics 3: Implement is a technical subject focused on construction drawings, details and documentations. We were given a schematic design that we were tasked to develop into construction drawings.

Location

524 Flinders Street.

Concept sketches provided by the school. We were given a ground floor plan, typical floor plan and a section. These concept sketches are to be further developed into proper construction drawings. Besides developing the design further, we are tasked to find errors and to comply to Australian Architecture code. Studio tutors act as the cient and we are to correspond with them regarding design changes and adjustments.

Starting point for adjusting the mass of the concept skectches. These site diagrams adjusts how the concept sketch form was intially to optimize the radiation, wind and daylight the building captured or shaded from.s

We were tasked to incorporate a hollowcore floor system and too depict how the primary structural elements supports its own weight in addition to incorporating the hollowcore technology.

Fire escape diagram and compliance to the Fire code. The concept sketches had to be modified heavily to comply with the code. Although the position of the staircases were perfect I had to amend it to allow for compliance without hindering the useability of the main spaces and not be in the way for the users.

The plans were developed in conjuction to the type of space and how each space could potentially affect each other. Accessibility was key, making sure ambulant individuals had the same experience as the abled. Materiality was also an important factor in making sure that each space felt different.

The elevation and section drawings were developed in conjuction with the studio tutor or 'client'. I proposed a brick cladding design which harkons back to the locality and personality located in Flinders Street. My approach was to always incorporate the 'personality' of the locality back into the overall design as it gives the structure more 'soul' & even re-imagine how this could be implemented. Furthmore, the importance of correspondance between client and architect in the development of the finalization of the design is key.

These drawings bring to life the 'how?' question in architectural design and with the aid of the part elevation and section, it clarifies how the building is put together. For example, the Flexbrick system implementation as a second skin for the building to shade the internal spaces and how it fits with the main structure. Possible weak points where water might enter and waterproofing detail that shows how to mitigte this.

This axonometric part section shows the details for the joinery, windows, planter box and ground beam interface with one another.

The Final Renders

The final renders of the exterior and interior shows the final realization from the design sketch and the possibility of the GalleryHaus. GalleryHaus is a 4 story structure with the ground floor being an art gallery. The interior render showcases the possible works that could be on display and how people could use the space.
